Open Positions

Explore each department, its responsibilities, and what we expect from new members.

Human Resources (HR)

The people engine of the Hive.

Responsibilities

  • Organize recruitment campaigns and interviews.
  • Onboard new members and track engagement.
  • Conduct feedback surveys and 1:1 check-ins.
  • Support team-building and internal events.

Required skills

  • Strong communication and empathy.
  • Organization and follow-up.
  • Discretion and professionalism.

Expected workload

Approx. 3–5 hours per week, with peaks during recruitment and major events.

Finance

Keeping the Hive sustainable and growing.

Responsibilities

  • Prepare and track the club’s budget.
  • Manage expenses and reimbursements.
  • Support sponsorship and partnership proposals.
  • Report financial status to the bureau.

Required skills

  • Basic understanding of budgeting or willingness to learn.
  • Attention to detail and reliability.
  • Comfort with spreadsheets and documentation.

Expected workload

Approx. 2–4 hours per week, more around big events and sponsorship cycles.

Problem Setting

Designing challenges that push members to think deeper.

Responsibilities

  • Create and curate problems for contests and training sessions.
  • Test and validate problem difficulty and clarity.
  • Collaborate with event organizers on formats and scoring.

Required skills

  • Strong analytical and problem-solving mindset.
  • Interest in competitive programming or logic puzzles.
  • Ability to explain solutions clearly.

Expected workload

Approx. 3–6 hours per week, depending on contest schedule.

Design

Crafting the visual identity of the Hive.

Responsibilities

  • Create posters, social media visuals, and event branding.
  • Maintain a consistent visual identity across platforms.
  • Collaborate with Social Media and Events teams.

Required skills

  • Basic experience with design tools (Figma, Canva, Photoshop, etc.).
  • Sense of aesthetics and attention to detail.
  • Openness to feedback and iteration.

Expected workload

Approx. 3–5 hours per week, more during campaign periods.

Social Media

Telling the Hive’s story to the world.

Responsibilities

  • Plan and schedule posts on social platforms.
  • Write captions and short-form content.
  • Cover events live (photos, stories, recaps).
  • Monitor engagement and respond to messages.

Required skills

  • Good writing and communication.
  • Understanding of social media trends.
  • Consistency and responsiveness.

Expected workload

Approx. 3–5 hours per week, with flexibility around events.
